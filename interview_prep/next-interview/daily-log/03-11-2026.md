## 📚 What I Learned

- **ASC vs DESC in window functions** — for first record use `ORDER BY date ASC`; always ask "am I looking for first or last?"
- **LAG() ordering** — partition must be ordered chronologically (ASC) for time differences to make sense forward in time
- **DATE_TRUNC() in GROUP BY** — must include `DATE_TRUNC()` expression in GROUP BY, not just SELECT; forgetting this is a very common mistake
- **Spark skew — Key Salting** — add a random salt to distribute a skewed key across multiple partitions
- **Spark skew — Broadcast Join** — send small table to every executor to avoid shuffle entirely
- **Spark skew — AQE (Spark 3+)** — set `spark.sql.adaptive.enabled = true` to let Spark auto-detect and split skewed partitions
- **Late arriving data** — Kafka ensures delivery but does NOT solve late data; use reprocessing windows, MERGE/upsert, and watermarking instead
- **Data Vault dual timestamps** — Raw Vault captures both `LOAD_DATE` (when it arrived) and `EFFECTIVE_DATE` (actual business date)
